What is Image Metadata (EXIF)?

When you take a photo, your camera or smartphone embeds hidden information called EXIF data. This can include:

  • GPS Coordinates: Exactly where the photo was taken.
  • Camera Info: Make, model, and serial number of your device.
  • Settings: Shutter speed, aperture, ISO, and focal length.
  • Date and Time: Exactly when the photo was captured.
  • Software: The editing software used on the image.

By removing this data before sharing images online, you protect your personal information and location privacy.
